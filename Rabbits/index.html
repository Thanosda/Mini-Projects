Rabbits

Rabbits are small, furry mammals known for their long ears and powerful hind legs. They are herbivores, feeding mainly on grass and leafy plants. Rabbits live in burrows and are social animals, often found in groups. Their gentle nature and playful behavior make them popular pets and symbols of innocenc. Rabbits are fascinating creatures found in many parts of the world. They have soft fur, twitching noses, and large, expressive eyes. Their long ears help them detect predators, while their strong hind legs allow them to hop quickly and escape danger.



Rabbits are mostly active during dawn and dusk, a behavior known as crepuscular. They communicate using body language and gentle thumping of their feet. In the wild, rabbits dig complex burrow systems called warrens for shelter and protection. As prey animals, they rely on their agility and alertness to survive.
